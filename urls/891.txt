Prior to this, Wen served as the Venture Start Director and COO where she managed the operation and financials of EPICentre since the inception of EPICentre in 2014. Wen started her career with the University of Windsor as a Research Commercialization Associate in 2012 after obtaining her MBA from the Odette School of Business. Wen has strong entrepreneurship in her DNA. She grew up in an entrepreneurial family and has started two businesses before joining the University of Windsor. She co-founded an international gas distribution company in Malaysia and a high-pressure cylinder distribution company in the U.S. Currently, Wen is also a sessional instructor at the Odette School of Business, and a Board member at the WindsorEssex Community Foundation. Sydney Thompson EPIC Program & Event Lead Sydney is an Honours graduate from the University of Windsor in Business Administration with a concentration in entrepreneurship & strategy. She also holds a three-year college diploma for Human Resource Management from St. Clair College. Sydney began her EPICentre journey during her final semester as an undergraduate student at the University of Windsor. She participated in the EPIC Odette Technology consulting course – an international multidisciplinary course in collaboration with Oxford Brookes University. Concurrently, Sydney also conducted a research project for the EPIC Industrial Hub, examining academic and non-academic makerspaces with reference to their health and safety policies and procedures. Sydney previously worked as a program coordinator at St. Clair College in the Genesis Entrepreneurship Centre. Currently, Sydney oversees the activities, programs and events for EPICentre as the Program & Event Lead. Paul Brereton Program Lead - Emerging Tech Paul is a Research Associate who works with entrepreneurs to promote growth, evolution, and inclusivity in emerging technologies. He holds an MBA as well as an Honours Bachelor of Commerce from The University of Windsor. Paul believes that putting tools, resources, and know-how into the hands of entrepreneurs, especially those in a university setting, is one of the best ways to promote innovation and economic growth; with these advantages reaching from small, student-led start-ups, all the way to major corporations. As a founder and CEO of a Windsor based analytics firm that uses machine learning, artificial intelligence, and advanced quantitative methodology to help area businesses better leverage their data, Paul has first-hand experience using these entrepreneurship principals to build a stronger, more innovative community. Paul started his adventure with the EPICentre as a member of both the Discovery and RBC EPIC Founder’s programs. Through these opportunities with the EPICentre, Paul was able to integrate his educational background with real, on the ground testing and validating of his business model. He is excited for the opportunity to share his wealth of skills and experience with future generations of entrepreneurs. EPIC Strategic Researchers Professor Myra Tawfik Professor of Law and EPICentre Professor of IP Commercialization & Strategy Professor Tawfik is a professor at the Faculty of Law with expertise in intellectual property law including comparative and international aspects of IP as well as IP strategy. She has founded and led a number of intellectual property clinical initiatives at the University of Windsor, starting with the Intellectual Property Legal Information Network (IPLIN) in 2004. In 2016, she was appointed EPICentre Professor of IP Commercialization & Strategy to continue her research, teaching and IP outreach activities. Professor Tawfik is also a Senior Fellow at the Centre for International Governance Innovation (CIGI). Dr. Francine Schlosser Odette Professor in Entrepreneurship and Innovation Dr. Schlosser has worked collaboratively to establish a multi-disciplinary entrepreneurial culture. For more than 10 years she has engaged students with the business community through multi-disciplinary venture teams and consulting projects and has established 3 successive entrepreneurship centres on campus. Previously, Dr.Schlosser held the position of Executive Director at the University of Windsor EPICentre. She holds the University of Windsor’s Golden Jubilee Professorship at the Odette School of Business, awarded for research excellence, most recently in career transition and entrepreneurial readiness. She is a member of the Board of Directors for Windsor-Essex Chamber of Commerce and Workforce Windsor Essex. She was previously Board Secretary, Canadian Council of Small Business and Entrepreneurship, and is on the Editorial Board for the Journal of Small Business and Entrepreneurship. More info: http://www1.uwindsor.ca/odette/francine-schlosser Dr. Gerry Kerr Associate Professor, Odette School of Business Dr. Gerry Kerr received a Ph.D. in Strategic Management from the Schulich School of Business at York University in Toronto, Canada. Gerry has taught full-time since 2001 when he joined the Odette School of Business, and his specialities include Strategy and Entrepreneurship. Since then, Dr. Kerr has gratefully received multiple teaching awards from undergraduate, graduate, and executive students. Dr. Kerr’s research in entrepreneurship includes the examination of resource development and performance in small firms, as well as projects focused on aspects of ethnic/immigrant and older entrepreneurs. Gerry has also served as M.B.A. Program Director and as Area Chair, Strategy & Entrepreneurship at the Odette School of Business. EPIC University Advisors Heather Pratt Executive Director, Research and Innovation With close to 20 years of experience in progressive research administration, Heather has played a leading role in the development of the University of Windsor’s research enterprise and expansion. During her tenure, the University’s research funding levels have nearly doubled and its research mandate has broadened significantly to sustain increasing support for technology transfer, research partnerships, knowledge mobilization and entrepreneurship. Heather is a founding Director of the University of Windsor’s EPICentre.
